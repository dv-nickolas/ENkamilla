My Experience in the Student Council:
Three Months of Meaningful Work

“ ”
Although I have only been part of the Student Council for three months, I have already had the chance to contribute in a meaningful way to its work.

HSE Situation Center

Nearly a month of daily volunteering at the HSE Situation Center became one of the most challenging — yet one of the most valuable — periods for me. The work was intense, requiring focus, quick decision-making, and emotional resilience.

The support provided by the administration and by us, the volunteers, was highly appreciated by hundreds of international students who faced difficulties during their adaptation. We handled calls and provided guidance on migration cards, documentation, and registration procedures.

Committee for Work With International Students

As part of the committee, I organized the first informal student meetup, which included various activities aimed at helping international students connect and integrate into the university community. The event featured interactive games, cultural exchange, and a quest.

Together, our committee has developed ambitious plans for new initiatives aimed at improving student life — including a mentoring program, cultural events, meet-ups, discussions, and shared activities.

Secretariat Work

My role in the Secretariat, although less public, is essential to the Council’s day-to-day functioning. I am responsible for organizational, analytical, and advisory support within the Council.

My Motivation:
Why I Want to Be Part of the Student Council

“ ”
Three key reasons
that inspire me every single day…

Meaningful Interaction With People

For me, communication is not just a part of the work — it is its foundation. In the Student Council, I hope to find like-minded people who share my values and ambitions. Together, we can grow, strengthen our skills, and create a synergy that benefits the entire university community.

Every meeting, every project, every conversation is an opportunity to learn something new, hear another perspective, and grow both as a person and as a leader.

Improving the System

I understand that perfection does not exist — but striving for greater quality and productivity should be continuous. For me, this is a personal challenge: to improve certain aspects of how things work, make processes more efficient, and make student life more comfortable.

This is not about criticizing the current system, but about contributing to its evolution. Every organization needs fresh ideas and new perspectives, and I am ready to offer mine.

Mutual Support

Last on the list, but first in importance, is helping others. Mutual support is something everyone needs at some point in life. As a candidate — regardless of the election results — I am ready to provide that support. I want to use all available resources — my time, energy, knowledge, and connections — to help the dedicated students of the HSE Business School. Whether it’s assistance with adaptation, academic guidance, support in organizing an event, or simply a friendly conversation — I am here for you.
